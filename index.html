Study Overview

This supplemental webpage includes detailed methodological visuals and comprehensive datasets from our comparative study examining the pressure-volume characteristics of endotracheal tube (ETT) cuffs across rigid, elastic, and cadaveric airway models.

Main Article (submitted): Characterization of Endotracheal Tube Cuff Pressure-Volume in Simulated and Cadaveric Tracheas

About This Study: This poster and supplemental webpage detail our investigation into the pressure-volume relationships of endotracheal tube (ETT) cuffs in different airway models. Our goal was to highlight how cuff design and airway compliance influence safe inflation margins, and to emphasize the clinical necessity of precise cuff pressure management. Through rigorous testing with rigid, elastic, and cadaveric models, we demonstrate that even small volume increments can significantly elevate cuff pressures, potentially leading to patient harm. This underscores the importance of objective cuff pressure monitoring in clinical practice, moving beyond standard estimation techniques.
